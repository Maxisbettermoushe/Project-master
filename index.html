My diaganoses

Johanessburg, South Africa

  • I was diagnosed with Type One diabetes on August 2nd 2004, just twenty two days before my fourth birthday. Due the rampant poverty of South Africa the healthcare system wasnâ€™t the best, but fornatantly my family was fairly well off so I had the best available care. When I was diagnosed I was given the prognosis of a 30 year life span.

Process of learning how to be diabetic

Johanessburg, South Africa

  • My life completely changed after I was diagnosed, as the amount of food I could eat, when I could eat and injections became a daily hassle. To this day when I recall my memories of South Africa, I remember how my mother chasing me down to jab me with syringes of insulin or how I was devastated by not being able to eat the cake at birthday parties because I had already consumed my daily allotment of carbohydrates. The transition into being a diabetic was confusing as I too young to fully comprehend what it fully meant. All I knew was that I hated being Diabetic.

First Pump

Johanessburg, South Africa

  • Fortunately, before I moved to England in 2005. My doctor recommended that I was to be placed on a diabetic pump, as at this time, they were just starting to become commercially available yet in England the M.H.R.A. (British F.D.A.) only allowed adults access to pumps. The doctor recommended me to get a pump since they were easier to manage and less intrusive compared to injections.

Bullying

Manchester, England

  • Throughout my experience of being a diabetic, I can recall several times where I was singled out for being Diabetic. I can remember the disappointment of not getting invited for sleepovers because parents were afraid to manage a diabetic kid or the time I hid in the bathroom stall when the boys at soccer camp made fun of me because I had to stop playing and adjust my sugar level. The most shocking was the time I was finished when I went through security at an international airport because of suspicions about the electronic device attached to my body (my pump).
